Overview

Structure, flow, and interaction.

Curse Of Osiris is a prototyping based on Egyptian mythology. The concept of the game is mainly inspired from Hades. Game design concentrating heavily on game mechanics and narration.

As a core member of this group project, my mission was to architect an intense, finely-tuned gameplay slice. Every corridor, every open arena, and every chokepoint was deliberately engineered to test the player's reflexes and tactical adaptability, resulting in a fiercely playable experience.

Role & Contribution

Architecting the experience.

  • Prototyping & Whiteboxing: Spearheaded the initial blockouts, translating abstract gameplay concepts into tangible, perfectly-scaled 3D spaces that naturally guide player movement and combat flow.
  • Asset Implementation: Meticulously placed environmental assets to build a cohesive aesthetic while establishing clear sightlines, cover spots, and focal points that subconsciously drive navigation.
  • Gameplay Scripting: Engineered robust scripting for core mechanics, dynamic encounters, and interactive elements, ensuring a flawless, responsive gameplay loop.
  • UI Elements: Designed and implemented critical UI components that communicate essential information clearly, keeping the player deeply immersed in the action without unnecessary clutter.
Player Experience

Mythology meets mechanics.

Blending the rich thematic backdrop of Egyptian mythology with rapid, Hades-inspired combat pacing, the player experience is designed to be both narratively engaging and mechanically demanding. The environment and the narrative feed directly into the gameplay loop.

By intricately balancing heavy mechanical design with deep narration, the level breathes rhythmically—oscillating between high-tension combat arenas and meaningful narrative moments. The result is a visceral, deeply satisfying loop where the player's skill and engagement with the story are the ultimate focal points.
