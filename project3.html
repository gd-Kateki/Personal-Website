Overview

🗺️ Map Overview & Identity

  • Map Name: 742 Blackwood Lane
  • Map Size: Small to Medium (Single Floor + Workshop Extension)
  • Difficulty Rating: Medium
  • Unique Trait: A sprawling, linear layout that transitions from a classic family home into an isolated, narrow workshop area. While it offers excellent looping zones, its literal "dead ends" make early hunts incredibly punishing if you get caught out of position.

The Briefing Log

"We’ve been called to a recently inherited property on Blackwood Lane. The current owner, a relative of a reclusive local carpenter, reports hearing erratic footsteps moving from the main dining hall to the rear workshops late at night. Neighbors claim the old man became obsessively paranoid in his final months, boarding up windows and rearranging furniture to create 'barriers.' Look out for sudden temperature drops, and remember—that workshop out back only has one way in, and one way out."
Level Breakdown

🛠️ Level Breakdown & Phasmophobia Gameplay Integration

Based on your design files HH1.jpg, HH2.jpg, HH3.jpg, and HH4.jpg, here is how the mechanics of a ghost hunt map play out on this floor plan.

1. Entrances & Exits (Red Squares)

As marked in HH1.jpg, the house features two key choke points:

  • Main Entrance (Front): Opens directly into the primary living spaces (HH2.jpg). This is where the investigation team will spawn outside with the truck.
  • Side/Rear Exit: Located near the central hallway junction. If the ghost initiates a hunt while you are investigating the back rooms, this door will lock instantly, forcing you to utilize the surrounding loops.

2. Loopable Objects & Safe Escape Zones (Red Circles/Ovals)

This map provides solid counterplay for experienced investigators looking to smudge or outrun the ghost during a hunt:

  • The Grand Dining Table: Visible clearly in HH2.jpg, HH3.jpg, and HH4.jpg. This long table over the oriental rug is your primary, safest looping object in the center of the house.
  • The Formal Sunroom / Sitting Area: Located in the bottom-right corner of HH1.jpg and shown from an angle in HH2.jpg. The circular coffee table arrangement allows for tight, tense looping if the ghost traps you in the front wing.
  • Central Hallway / Living Room Dividing Wall: An elongated loop zone in the middle of the house (HH1.jpg) that lets you break line of sight using the structural pillars and partial walls.
  • The Parallel Lockers / Hallway Closets: A row of narrow rooms shown in HH1.jpg, HH3.jpg, and HH4.jpg. These serve as excellent Safe Escapes, offering multiple doors to duck into to hide and break line-of-sight.

3. The Danger Zones: Dead Ends (Blue Squares)

If you are caught in these areas when the ghost manifests or hunts, your survival chances drop drastically unless you have a Crucifix or a Smudge Stick ready.

  • The Isolated Workshop (Top Blue Square): This deep back-room extension (visible on the far left of HH4.jpg and top of HH1.jpg) is a literal death trap. It features a completely linear entrance with zero looping geometry. If the ghost room is back here, investigators will have to play incredibly cautiously.
  • The Front Sunroom Corner (Bottom Blue Square): While it contains a loopable circular table (HH2.jpg), the surrounding glass walls and structural corner mean you can easily run out of room if a fast ghost (like a Thaye or Moroi) corners you.
Item Spawns

🕯️ Cursed Possession Spawns

To fit the authentic Phasmophobia loop, here is where the Cursed Possessions will randomly generate across your level layout:

Cursed Item Spawn Location Gameplay Context
Ouija Board On the long Dining Table (HH3.jpg). Risky to use in the open, but gives you an immediate escape route around the table if it triggers a hide-and-seek hunt.
Haunted Mirror Mounted on the wall in the narrow parallel closet hallway (HH4.jpg). Allows you to peer into the ghost room, but drains sanity rapidly in a cramped space.
Monkey Paw Resting on the fireplace mantle in the main living room (HH2.jpg). Right near the front entrance for quick, desperate wishes.
Tarot Cards On the small circular coffee table in the sunroom (HH2.jpg). High risk, high reward in a potential dead-end zone.
Music Box On the standalone shelf in the center hallway break-room (HH1.jpg). Can be used to draw the ghost out into the main dining room loop.
Summoning Circle Right in the center of the isolated Back Workshop (HH4.jpg). The ultimate challenge. Lighting this guarantees you must sprint down a long, narrow corridor to get back to the safe zones of the house before the hunt begins.
Gameplay Flow

👻 Investigative Strategy for 742 Blackwood Lane

  • Early Game: Move quickly through the front living spaces to check the Dining Room and Sunroom for early freezing temperatures or EMF readings.
  • Mid Game: If the ghost is residing in the back corridor or workshop (HH4.jpg), position a Crucifix at the workshop threshold. This prevents the ghost from spawning in a position that entirely cuts off your only path of retreat.
  • Late Game (During Hunts): If you hear the front door lock, do not panic and run to the back workshop. Instead, draw the ghost toward the Dining Room Table or slip into one of the three parallel closets to wait out the hunt in silence.
